Introduction about the company

BMW, or Bayerische Motoren Werke AG, is a renowned German automotive company founded in 1916 in Munich,Germany. Originally an aircraft engine manufacturer during World War I, BMW transitioned to motorcycles in 1917 and automobiles in 1928 with the introduction of the BMW Dixi. Today, BMW is a global leader in luxury automobile manufacturing, known for precision engineering, cutting-edge technology, and a commitment to "sheer driving pleasure." The company has manufacturing facilities worldwide, producing a diverse range of vehicles, including electric models under the "BMW i" sub-brand. With over a century of history, BMW remains an iconic symbol of German engineering and automotive excellence.

Karl Rapp (1882-1962)


Karl Rapp was a German engineer and entrepreneur who played a pivotal role in the early days of BMW. He founded the Rapp Motorenwerke company in 1913, which specialized in aircraft engines. Later, in 1916, Rapp merged his company with Gustav Otto's aircraft engine factory, forming Bayerische Motoren Werke AG, or BMW. While Rapp was instrumental in the company's early development, he eventually left BMW, and the company evolved into the world-renowned automobile manufacturer we know today. Rapp's contributions laid the foundation for BMW's future success in precision engineering and advanced technology.
